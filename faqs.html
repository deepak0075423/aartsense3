General

Aart Sense Studios is a London-based creative studio founded by multi-award-winning artist, composer, and filmmaker Arjuna Harjai. We specialise in original music composition, sync licensing, brand sound strategy, music supervision for film and television, and artist development through the Aart Sense Academy.
Arjuna Harjai is a BAFTA-shortlisted, ABBY Gold-winning artist and filmmaker with over two decades of experience. He has scored music for international brands including Dettol, Amazon, Google, Cadbury, Jio, and Pepsi; composed for Bollywood films including P.O.W. – Baandi Ya Aaraad and Lucknow Central; and charted on Spotify's Viral 50 (UK) and BBC Asian Network's Top 30. He is the founder and creative director of Aart Sense Studios.
Our registered office is at 128 City Road, London, EC1V 2NX, United Kingdom. We work with clients across India, Europe, North America, and the Middle East on a remote and in-person basis.
Our catalogue spans Hindustani classical, contemporary Bollywood, acoustic folk, ambient cinematic, electronic, jazz-fusion, and bespoke brand scores. Arjuna's versatility means we rarely turn down a brief based on genre — if anything, mixing aesthetics is where our best work tends to come from.
The fastest route is via our Contact page. For general enquiries email mail@aartsense.co.uk. For urgent or time-sensitive briefs, indicate "URGENT" in the subject line and we will aim to respond within 4 hours during business hours (Mon–Fri, 9am–6pm GMT).

Booking & Process

Start by submitting a brief via the Contact page or emailing us directly. Include your project type (ad, film, brand, personal), the mood or reference tracks you have in mind, your intended use (broadcast, digital, social), and your deadline. We will schedule a discovery call within 48 hours to align on creative direction before beginning any work.
Turnaround depends heavily on the scope. A 30-second brand jingle can be delivered in 5–7 working days. A full film score typically requires 4–12 weeks. For advertising campaigns with multiple assets, we normally agree milestones at the brief stage. We always recommend building in a minimum of 10 working days for any commissioned project.
Yes. Standard commissions include two rounds of creative revisions. Additional revision rounds can be agreed at an hourly rate before work begins. We find that detailed briefs and an upfront alignment call significantly reduce the need for revisions, so we invest time in that process from the start.
Absolutely — and we encourage it for larger projects. For remote clients, we offer a live session stream with a director's audio feed so you can provide real-time feedback. For clients who can travel to London, studio visits can be arranged with advance notice.
We deliver broadcast-quality WAV or AIFF (24-bit / 48kHz or 96kHz as required) as standard, along with MP3 (320kbps) for reference. For broadcast and film, we can provide stems, M&E tracks, and cue sheets on request. Video-synced deliveries (Quicktime or MXF) are also available for post-production workflows.

Licensing & Rights

For bespoke commissions, the copyright structure is agreed in the contract before work begins. Typically, Aart Sense retains the master and publishing rights while granting the client a broad synchronisation licence. Full buyouts — where all rights transfer to the client — are available at a higher fee. We never assign rights without a signed agreement in place.
Yes. We have a growing catalogue of cleared, sync-ready tracks available for licensing across advertising, digital content, film, and TV. Send us your brief — territory, media type, duration, and exclusivity requirements — and we will provide a quote within 24 hours.
Yes. Our music supervision service includes full clearance management — we identify rights holders, negotiate sync and master licences, and handle all paperwork. This is particularly useful for advertising agencies and post-production houses who need to clear music quickly without the back-and-forth of direct negotiation.
We can license for any territory or combination of territories — UK only, pan-European, South Asia, worldwide, or custom. Licence fees are scaled accordingly. We regularly handle India-UK cross-territory briefs and are comfortable navigating both PRS (UK) and IPRS (India) frameworks.

Pricing

Pricing is project-specific and depends on scope, usage, duration, and exclusivity. As a guide: a 30-second social ad score starts from £1,500; a full 60-second brand TVC score from £3,500; a feature film score is quoted on application. We are transparent about costs and will always provide a detailed quote before work begins — no hidden fees.
Yes. We have a limited number of reduced-rate slots each quarter for independent and emerging projects we believe in creatively. If you are a first-time filmmaker, independent label, or early-stage brand, mention it in your initial enquiry and we will let you know if we can accommodate your budget.
Standard terms are 50% deposit on signing, 50% on delivery. For larger projects (£10,000+), we can structure milestone payments. We accept bank transfer (BACS / SWIFT), and invoice in GBP or INR. Invoices are issued within one business day of each payment stage.
Session musician fees, studio hire (if external), and third-party clearance costs are quoted separately if applicable. If we need to bring in additional live musicians beyond our in-house ensemble, we will always get your approval before incurring those costs. Our quotes are always all-inclusive of our own creative and production fees.

The Academy

The Aart Sense Academy is our artist development and music education division. It offers one-to-one mentorship, group masterclasses, and intensive programmes covering songwriting, music production, film scoring, and the business of music. Courses are delivered online and in-person in London.
Our programmes cater to a wide range — from complete beginners exploring music for the first time, to professional musicians looking to transition into sync, film, or brand music. Each intake is assessed individually so we can ensure you are placed in the right programme.
Applications open three times a year (January, May, and September). Submit an expression of interest via the Contact page selecting "Academy Enquiry" from the subject dropdown. We will send you the current course catalogue and application form. Intake is limited to ensure quality of mentorship.
Yes. We offer two partial-scholarship places per intake for candidates from underrepresented backgrounds in the music industry. Scholarship applications are reviewed by Arjuna personally. Details are shared with the full course catalogue on application.
